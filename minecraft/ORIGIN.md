# ORIGIN
### The Lineage of Antithesis

---

## Cosmos — The Unrealized Game

Four years ago, two co-owners of **Cosmos Enterprises** conceived a game called **Cosmos**.

It was an ambitious synthesis:
- Movement and combat inspired by **Ultrakill**
- Visual style drawn from **Celeste**
- Gameplay elements from **The Messenger**
- Narrative depth leaning toward **Undertale**

The genre was a **Hollow Knight-style action platformer.**

### The Player Character — Cosmos

Cosmos was a space-person in a **purple astronaut suit** — identity fully obscured. Tall, skinny, sharp. Armed with a full arsenal of **purple space weapons**. The suit and silhouette were the character; what was underneath was never the point.

### The World

Cosmos crash-landed on a planet with **7 main areas**, each governed by a boss. The areas were not just levels — they were **extensions of each boss's personality and character**. The world was built from the inside out: understand the boss, understand their territory.

The goal: ascend Cosmos Mountain to escape — either to repair the ship or simply to get out.

Cosmos was never fully realized. The scope of the vision outgrew its moment. But the characters, the world, and the intent were real — and they deserve to exist in some form.

---

## Antithesis — The Character

Antithesis was the **penultimate boss** of Cosmos — Area 6, the last major obstacle before **THE PLANT** and the final ascent.

### Its Area

Antithesis's territory was designed to be genuinely hostile — not dangerous in a designed, gamified way, but deeply, atmospherically *wrong*:

- **Shattered glass** — everywhere, inescapable
- **Muted, damp greens** — sickly, organic, suffocating
- **The moon** — ever-present, watching
- **Metal and toxicity** — industrial rot meeting biological decay
- No comfort. No reprieve. Truly an unfun place to be — and that was the point.

### Its Nature

Antithesis is a **shapeshifter** — fully, completely. Its identity is defined by the absence of a fixed one. It becomes whatever it needs to be. It does not have a true form because it does not need one.

For the fight against Cosmos, it chose its form deliberately: **a warped mirror of Cosmos itself.** Same silhouette. Same proportions. But wrong — distorted, twisted, a reflection seen through broken glass. It carried technological augmentations: a **robot arm** and **mechanical wings** — corrupted versions of tools, weaponized.

Design inspiration was drawn from **Nosk** in Hollow Knight — the horror of facing something that wears a familiar shape.

### Its Defeat

Cosmos kills Antithesis. And in doing so, **takes the robot arm and mechanical wings for themselves** — claiming the enemy's power as their own. This is where Antithesis's story ends in the original Cosmos narrative.

### What Made It Work

Antithesis was designed to be the peak of threat — the most powerful, most menacing, most terrifying thing Cosmos had encountered up to that point. A force of nature in the truest sense: not evil by intent, not hostile by design, but fundamentally incompatible with Cosmos's survival. It did not pursue. It did not taunt. It simply *was*, and that was enough.

---

## Antithesis Conquering Simulator — The Extension

Cosmos was never finished. This game is not a sequel or a reboot. It is an **extension** — a way to honor the vision and get the ball rolling on what Cosmos Enterprises was reaching toward.

The choice to build around Antithesis specifically is intentional:

- Antithesis was defined enough to have an identity but never got to *be* anything beyond a boss encounter
- Its kit — the robot arm, the mechanical wings — is already canonically established as a player loadout
- Its nature as a shapeshifter and force of nature translates directly to the no-narrative, pure-combat tone of this game
- It is the one Cosmos character that can exist *without* Cosmos

The weapons Cosmos claimed from Antithesis's corpse are now Antithesis's own — reclaimed, native, never borrowed. The **Laser Arm**, the **Mech Wings**, the **Flexible Left Arm** are not gear. They are anatomy.

---

## What This Means for the Game's Identity

Antithesis is not a new character. It is a character finally getting to exist on its own terms.

The game does not reference Cosmos. There are no easter eggs, no meta-narrative winks. This is not a love letter with footnotes — it is Antithesis doing what Antithesis does, in a context built around letting it do that fully.

But internally, every design decision carries the weight of what Cosmos was reaching for: **intensity, weirdness, a world that feels like it was built from personality outward, and a combat experience that honors Ultrakill's kinetic standard.**

Cosmos Enterprises started something. This is part of finishing it.
